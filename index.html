Academic & University Projects

Key technical projects completed during my Telecommunications Engineering degree.

Hardware and Software co-design

Laboratory course focused on the co-design and integration of hardware and software in FPGA-based systems. Developed multiple projects using Verilog and C on Intel DE2/DE1-SoC boards, from peripheral design and bus communication to embedded control and hardware acceleration for neural networks.

  • Toaster controller in C (state machines, interrupts, and display control)
  • Custom timer in Verilog (Avalon-MM interface and interrupt support)
  • Hardware acceleration of a backpropagation algorithm (ALUs, DMA, HLS)
Quartus II Verilog ModelSim Nios II C/C++

High-Frequency Electronics

Laboratory course focused on RF and microwave circuit design, including passive networks, low-noise amplifiers (LNAs), and oscillators. Projects were developed using AWR Microwave Office and involved the complete workflow from schematic to EM simulation and optimization.

  • Design and EM simulation of a 5th-order Chebyshev low-pass filter (lumped and microstrip implementations).
  • LNA design for Wi-Fi at 2.45 GHz: stability, noise/gain trade-off, and impedance matching.
  • Clapp oscillator at 2.5 GHz with phase noise optimization and transient/time-domain analysis.
Microwave Office S-Parameters Smith Chart LNA Oscillator Design Filter Design
